The Oil City

Takoradi, the other part of the twin city, Sekondi - Takoradi is a port city on the Gulf of Guinea (an embayment of the Atlantic Ocean) in Southern Ghana. Both the Dutch and the British built forts in the 17th century that were destroy by Ahanta, the natives of Takoradi. Fort Orange, rebuilt by the Dutch and bought by the British in 1872, survives as a lighthouse. The city is well planned with modern buildings and tree-shaded residential areas. The habour is terminus of several Ghana railwyas and is served by road and air.
